## Section 1: The Dashboard Fatigue Problem

[SCENE: Overhead view of a modern desk with multiple monitors showing CRM, email, project management, and analytics tools — all demanding attention simultaneously]

The average mid-size company now uses **112 different SaaS applications**. That number has tripled since 2018. Every one of those tools has its own dashboard, its own login, its own notification system competing for your team's attention.

But here's what nobody talks about — research from Harvard Business School shows that knowledge workers spend **28% of their workday** just switching between applications and re-establishing context. That's not productivity. That's digital busywork masquerading as progress.

The fundamental problem isn't any single tool. It's the architectural assumption that humans should be the integration layer between software systems.

## Section 2: How AI Agents Change the Equation

[SCENE: An elegant visualization showing an AI agent as a central hub, with data flowing in from multiple sources and actions being taken automatically — no dashboards needed]

AI agents flip the model entirely. Instead of you going to the software, the software comes to you — or more accurately, it handles things without involving you at all.

Take customer support as an example. Traditional approach: tickets come in through Zendesk, an agent triages them, escalates some, resolves others, updates the CRM, sends follow-ups. That's **five different systems** for one customer interaction.

An AI agent does all of that in a single execution loop. Companies like Intercom and Klarna report that their AI agents now resolve **82% of support conversations** without human intervention — and customer satisfaction scores actually went up.

The key insight is this: SaaS sells you **access to capabilities**. AI agents sell you **outcomes**. That's not an incremental improvement — it's a category shift.

## Section 3: The Industries Getting Disrupted First

[SCENE: An infographic showing three industry sectors — customer service, financial operations, and sales development — each with before/after metrics]

Three sectors are feeling this shift most acutely right now.

First, **customer service** — we just covered this. The $40 billion help desk market is contracting as AI agents absorb tier-1 and tier-2 support entirely.

Second, **financial operations**. AI agents are now handling invoice processing, expense categorization, and month-end reconciliation. Brex and Ramp have both shipped agents that reduce finance team workload by **60%** compared to traditional AP automation tools.

Third, and perhaps most surprisingly, **sales development**. AI agents are writing personalized outreach, qualifying leads, booking meetings, and maintaining CRM records — tasks that previously required both an SDR and three separate software tools. Early adopters report **3x pipeline growth** at half the headcount cost.
